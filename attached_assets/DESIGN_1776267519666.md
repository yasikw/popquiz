# Design System Strategy: The Playful Curator

## 1. Overview & Creative North Star
The "Playful Curator" is a design system that marries high-end editorial precision with the unbridled energy of "Pop" aesthetics. Moving away from the generic blue gradients of standard utility apps, this system embraces **Kinetic Softness**. 

Our Creative North Star is to create an experience that feels like a premium physical board game—tactile, high-quality, and deeply engaging. We break the "template" look by using intentional white space, exaggerated corner radii, and a "Tonal Stack" rather than rigid outlines. The interface should feel "bouncy" but stable, using a sophisticated pastel foundation to let vibrant accent colors drive user action without causing cognitive fatigue.

---

## 2. Colors: The Vibrant Tonal Palette
The palette is built on a warm, sun-drenched base, moving away from cold digital blues to a "Paper and Candy" color theory.

### Core Tones
*   **Surface Foundation (`#fdf6e3`):** This is our "Canvas." It’s warmer than pure white, providing a premium, eye-friendly background.
*   **Primary Accent (`#a8275a`):** A sophisticated deep pink for key brand moments.
*   **Secondary/Sunny Yellow (`#ffd709`):** Our energetic spark for success states and primary CTA highlights.
*   **Tertiary/Bright Mint (`#74f7f1`):** A cooling agent used for progress and balance.

### The Rules of Engagement
*   **The "No-Line" Rule:** Prohibit 1px solid borders for sectioning. Boundaries must be defined solely through background color shifts. For example, a `surface-container-low` card sitting on a `surface` background creates a natural edge. 
*   **Surface Hierarchy:** Treat the UI as stacked sheets of fine, thick paper. Use `surface-container-lowest` for the most elevated interactive elements (like quiz cards) and `surface-container-highest` for recessed "well" elements.
*   **The "Glass & Gradient" Rule:** Floating navigation or modal overlays must use Glassmorphism. Apply `surface` colors at 80% opacity with a `24px` backdrop-blur. 
*   **Signature Textures:** For high-impact CTAs, use a subtle linear gradient from `primary` (`#a8275a`) to `primary_container` (`#ff709f`) at a 135-degree angle. This adds a "soul" to the button that flat color cannot replicate.

---

## 3. Typography: Friendly Authority
We utilize **Plus Jakarta Sans** for its geometric yet friendly proportions. It offers a "rounded" feel without looking juvenile.

*   **Display Scale (Display-LG: 3.5rem):** Used sparingly for "Quiz Complete" or big score reveals. It should feel celebratory.
*   **Headline Scale (Headline-MD: 1.75rem):** For screen titles. We use a tighter letter-spacing (-0.02em) to give it a modern, editorial punch.
*   **Body Scale (Body-LG: 1rem):** High readability for quiz questions. Line height is set to 1.6 to ensure the "airy" feel of the system is maintained within text blocks.
*   **The Hierarchy Narrative:** Use `on-surface` for primary text and `on-surface-variant` for secondary metadata. Never use pure black (`#000000`) for text; we use `on-background` (`#322f22`) to maintain the warm, organic feel.

---

## 4. Elevation & Depth: Tonal Layering
Traditional dropshadows are often "muddy." In this system, depth is a product of light and color.

*   **The Layering Principle:** Place a `surface-container-lowest` card on a `surface-container-low` section. The slight shift in lightness creates a "soft lift."
*   **Ambient Shadows:** When a card needs to "float" (e.g., the active quiz question), use an extra-diffused shadow: `Y: 16px, Blur: 32px, Color: on-surface (8% opacity)`. This mimics natural sunlight rather than a digital glow.
*   **The "Ghost Border" Fallback:** If a border is required for accessibility, it must be the `outline-variant` token at 15% opacity. High-contrast 100% opaque borders are strictly forbidden.
*   **Bouncy Radii:** All interactive containers must use `xl` (3rem/48px) or `lg` (2rem/32px) rounding. This "bubble" aesthetic is the cornerstone of the system's "cute and pop" personality.

---

## 5. Components: Tactile & Energetic

### Buttons (The "Poppers")
*   **Primary:** Gradient from `primary` to `primary_container`. `xl` rounding. On hover, the shadow depth increases to simulate the button "rising" toward the user.
*   **Secondary:** `secondary_container` (`#ffd709`) with `on_secondary_container` text. This is your "Sunny Yellow" moment for high-energy actions like "Start Quiz."

### Cards (The "Plates")
*   Forbid divider lines. Use vertical white space (32px or 48px) to separate the question from the answers. 
*   Answer cards should use `surface-container-low`. Upon selection, they transition to `tertiary_container` (Mint) with a subtle "bounce" scale animation (1.02x).

### Inputs & Selectors
*   **Fields:** Use `surface-container-highest` for the input track to create a "recessed" look, making the text feel nestled within the UI.
*   **Checkboxes/Radios:** Oversized and rounded. When active, use a "Pop" animation where the inner dot expands from the center with an elastic easing curve.

### Progress Indicators
*   Avoid thin, clinical lines. Use thick, rounded tracks (`12px` height) with a gradient fill from `tertiary` to `tertiary_fixed`.

---

## 6. Do's and Don'ts

### Do:
*   **Do** use asymmetrical illustrative elements (like floating 3D shapes or organic blobs) in the background to break the grid.
*   **Do** use "Plus Jakarta Sans" in Semi-Bold for headers to maintain a "Pop" weight.
*   **Do** lean into the "Warmth"—ensure every surface has a hint of the yellow/cream base (`#fdf6e3`).

### Don't:
*   **Don't** use 1px solid borders. It shatters the "soft" illusion.
*   **Don't** use the old blue gradient. If a gradient is needed for a background, transition from `surface` to `surface_container_low`.
*   **Don't** clutter the screen. If you have more than 5 elements, use a "nested" container to group them, then use the Spacing Scale to let them breathe.
*   **Don't** use standard "Error Red." Use our `error` (`#b41340`) which is tuned to feel intentional within the warm palette.
